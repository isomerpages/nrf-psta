<h5><strong>President's Science &amp; Technology Medal (PSTM)</strong></h5><div class="isomer-image-wrapper"><img height="auto" width="100%" alt="President’s Science And Technology Medal (PSTM)" src="/images/Award%20Badges/badge-pstm.png"></div><p><br>Awarded to individuals who have made distinguished, sustained and exceptional contributions, and played a strategic role in advancing Singapore’s development through promotion and management of science and technology (S&amp;T).<br><br>The accomplishments and contributions would generally be acknowledged by the S&amp;T ecosystem as having a significant impact on the ecosystem’s capabilities or international stature. The PSTM is not meant to recognise research accomplishments or contributes to advancing individual technologies, as these are awarded under the PSA and PTA respectively. <br><br>Examples of achievements recognised by the PSTM include, but are not restricted to:<br>a. Being the driving force in pioneering the establishment of an important field of study in Singapore.<br>b. Being the leader who was instrumental in the raising of an S&amp;T institution to world-class status. <br>c. Contributing exceptionally to a major levelling-up of our S&amp;T ecosystem’s capabilities or international stature.<br><br><br></p><h5><strong>President's Science Award (PSA)</strong></h5><div class="isomer-image-wrapper"><img height="auto" width="100%" alt="President's Science Award (PSA)" src="/images/Award%20Badges/badge-pas.png"></div><p><br>Awarded to individuals or teams for outstanding contributions that have had a transformative effect on one or more S&amp;T fields. <br><br>Nominees are evaluated on:<br></p><ol data-tight="true" class="tight"><li><p><strong>Impact of Contributions</strong><br></p></li></ol><ul data-tight="true" class="tight"><li><p>Accomplishments and contributions are acknowledged by other S&amp;T practitioners as being significant and impactful to their field</p></li><li><p>Contributions need not have led to practical applications or products</p></li></ul><ol start="2" data-tight="true" class="tight"><li><p><strong>Includes, but is not restricted to, individual discoveries or a collective body of research work that has led to:</strong><br></p></li></ol><ul data-tight="true" class="tight"><li><p>Spawning or enabling new avenues of study</p></li><li><p>Revolutionising or significantly advancing the research methodology of a field</p></li><li><p>Significantly advancing or overturning our previous understanding of a field</p></li><li><p>Potential for significant scientific or industrial application <br><br><br></p></li></ul><h5><strong>President’s Technology Award (PTA)</strong></h5><div class="isomer-image-wrapper"><img height="auto" width="100%" alt="President’s Technology Award (PTA)" src="/images/Award%20Badges/badge-pta.png"></div><p><br>Awarded to individuals or teams for outstanding contributions that have led to transformative changes in the use or potential of technology. <br><br>Nominees are evaluated on:<br></p><ol data-tight="true" class="tight"><li><p><strong>Impact of Contributions</strong><br></p></li></ol><ul data-tight="true" class="tight"><li><p>Accomplishments and contributions are acknowledged by other S&amp;T practitioners as resulting from deep innovative input or ingenuity, marking a turning point for that technology’s use or potential, with significant impact on society</p></li><li><p>While the relevant technology need not be fully adopted or deployed at the point of nomination, it must have progressed beyond academic or theoretical potential, and be in the process of translation by industry or adoption by end-users</p></li></ul><ol start="2" data-tight="true" class="tight"><li><p><strong>Includes, but is not restricted to, individual breakthroughs or a collective body of R&amp;D work that has led to:</strong><br></p></li></ol><ul data-tight="true" class="tight"><li><p>Overcoming key obstacles that unlocked an impactful technology’s potential</p></li><li><p>Significantly advancing the state-of-the-art performance of an impactful technology</p></li><li><p>Innovative application of existing technology in a new, impactful way</p></li><li><p>Demonstrating a technology that disrupts the industry’s status quo <br><br><br></p></li></ul><h5><strong>Young Scientist Award (YSA)</strong></h5><div class="isomer-image-wrapper"><img height="auto" width="100%" alt="Young Scientist Awards (YSA)" src="/images/Award%20Badges/badge-yas.png"></div><p><br>Awarded to researchers aged 35 years and below, who are actively engaged in R&amp;D in Singapore, and who have shown great potential to be world-class researchers in their fields of expertise. This award is administered by the <a href="https://snas.org.sg/young-scientist-award" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">Singapore National Academy of Science (SNAS)</a> and supported by the National Research Foundation, Singapore (NRF). <br><br>There are two categories of Awards, namely:<br></p><ul data-tight="true" class="tight"><li><p>Biological and Biomedical Sciences; and</p></li><li><p>Physical, Information and Engineering Sciences <br><br></p></li></ul><h5><strong>Eligibility for the Awards</strong></h5><h5><strong>PSTM, PSA and PTA</strong><br></h5><p>Nominees must be outstanding contributors who have made an impact to Singapore’s R&amp;D landscape</p><ul data-tight="true" class="tight"><li><p>Singaporeans, Permanent Residents and foreigners may apply, as long as a substantial amount of research has been carried out in Singapore <br><em>(Note: most of the projects submitted have at least 50% of the work done in Singapore)</em></p></li><li><p>No age limit</p></li><li><p>Selected candidate(s) should still be active in research in the year the award is presented <br></p></li></ul><p></p>
